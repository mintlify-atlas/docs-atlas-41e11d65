The Ayurvedic Centers feature at `/dashboard/centers` helps you find qualified Ayurvedic practitioners and treatment facilities near you. Mydva's digital tools give you a strong foundation — a dosha profile, personalized consultation, and herbal guidance — but in-person care remains an important part of a complete Ayurvedic wellness practice. The center finder bridges the two.

## How to use the center finder

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your location">
    Type your city, region, or postal code into the search field. The finder uses your input to surface centers within a relevant radius.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse results">
    Results are displayed as a list with key details for each center. You can scroll through the list or, where map integration is available, view centers plotted on a map.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review a center">
    Select any result to expand its full details and contact information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make contact">
    Use the contact details provided to call, email, or visit the center's website to book an appointment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why in-person consultation matters

Mydva's AI consultation provides personalized guidance based on your self-reported data, but a qualified Ayurvedic practitioner brings capabilities that digital tools cannot replicate:

- **Pulse diagnosis (nadi pariksha)** — a practitioner reads your pulse to assess the state of your doshas and organ systems in ways that go beyond questionnaire-based analysis
- **Physical examination** — direct observation of your complexion, eyes, tongue, and physical build informs diagnosis
- **Dynamic adjustment** — an experienced practitioner can modify recommendations in real time based on how your body responds to treatment
- **Panchakarma supervision** — intensive detoxification and rejuvenation therapies must be administered and monitored by trained professionals

<Info>
Digital tools like Mydva are best understood as preparation and ongoing support — not as replacements for clinical Ayurvedic care.
</Info>

## Preparing for your in-person visit

Getting the most from an in-person Ayurvedic consultation is easier when you arrive with a clear picture of your health history and constitution.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Print or export your dosha analysis">
    Share your Vata, Pitta, and Kapha percentages with your practitioner. This gives them a baseline to compare against their own pulse and physical assessment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bring your consultation results">
    Your Mydva consultation report includes your condition analysis, dosha impact assessment, and current recommendations. A practitioner can use this as a starting point and refine or confirm the guidance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="List your current herbs and supplements">
    If you have already started any herbal remedies from your Mydva recommendations, bring that list so the practitioner can review it in the context of their own assessment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare your questions">
    Note any symptoms, concerns, or aspects of your consultation results you want the practitioner to clarify or expand on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Many Ayurvedic centers offer an initial consultation as a standalone appointment before recommending ongoing treatment. This is a good way to assess whether a practitioner's approach aligns with your needs before committing to a longer program.
</Tip>
