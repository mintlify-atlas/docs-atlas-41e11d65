The herbal remedy section at `/dashboard/herbal-remedy` gives you an interactive way to explore the herbs that appear in Ayurvedic practice and in your personal consultation results. Each herb is presented with a three-dimensional model you can inspect alongside detailed information about its properties, traditional uses, and how to prepare and use it safely.

## The 3D herb viewer

The viewer is powered by the `HerbModel3D` component, which renders a fully interactive three-dimensional model of each herb.

You can interact with the model in three ways:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Rotate">
    Click and drag to rotate the model in any direction. This lets you examine the herb from different angles — root, stem, leaf, and flower.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Zoom">
    Scroll or pinch to zoom in and out. Zooming in reveals finer details like leaf texture and surface features that are relevant to identification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inspect">
    Hover over or tap highlighted regions on the model to surface annotations about that part of the plant — for example, whether the root, leaf, or seed is the therapeutically active portion.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Herb information panels

Each herb entry in the explorer includes:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ayurvedic properties" icon="leaf">
    Rasa (taste), virya (potency), vipaka (post-digestive effect), and which doshas the herb pacifies or aggravates.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Traditional uses" icon="book">
    Classical Ayurvedic indications as described in texts such as the Charaka Samhita and Ashtanga Hridayam.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Preparation methods" icon="mortar-pestle">
    Common forms — powder (churna), decoction (kashaya), oil (taila), or paste (lepa) — with basic preparation notes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dosage and timing" icon="clock">
    General dosage ranges, timing relative to meals, and how dosage may vary by condition or constitution.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
Dosage and preparation details in the explorer reflect traditional Ayurvedic references. Your [personal consultation](/features/personal-consultation) provides dosage recommendations specifically calibrated to your dosha profile and health conditions, which may differ from general reference values.
</Note>

## Connection to your consultation plan

When your personal consultation recommends specific herbs, each herb name in the results links directly to its entry in the herbal remedy explorer. This allows you to:

- Examine the herb's 3D model before sourcing it
- Review its Ayurvedic properties and understand why it was recommended for your profile
- Check preparation instructions to ensure you are using it correctly

This connection between the consultation output and the explorer is intentional — understanding what a herb is and how to use it is as important as knowing it was recommended.

## Using herbal recommendations safely

Ayurvedic herbs are potent and, in some cases, contraindicated with certain medications or conditions. Before starting any herbal remedy:

<Warning>
Always consult a qualified Ayurvedic practitioner before beginning any herbal regimen, especially if you are pregnant, nursing, managing a chronic illness, or taking prescription medications. Some herbs can interact with pharmaceutical drugs or have dose-dependent effects that require professional oversight.
</Warning>

<Tip>
When visiting an Ayurvedic center or practitioner, bring your Mydva consultation results and the specific herbs listed in your plan. A practitioner can verify the recommendations are appropriate for your current state and advise on sourcing quality preparations.
</Tip>

General guidelines for safe use:

- Source herbs from reputable suppliers with quality verification (organic certification or GMP compliance where available)
- Start with the lower end of any dosage range and observe your body's response
- Discontinue use and consult a practitioner if you experience any unexpected reaction
- Do not self-escalate dosages beyond what is recommended in your consultation
