The education hub at `/dashboard/education` is a curated library of articles and videos designed to help you build a working understanding of Ayurvedic principles. Whether you are just beginning to explore Ayurveda or looking to deepen your knowledge of specific practices, the education section gives you the context to make better use of your dosha analysis, consultation results, and herbal guidance.

## Article library

Articles are presented using the `ArticleCard` component, which displays each piece with a title, summary, topic tag, and estimated reading time.

Topics covered in the article library include:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Dosha fundamentals" icon="book-open">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Diet and nutrition" icon="utensils">
    Ayurvedic dietary principles including the six tastes (shadrasa), seasonal eating, and dosha-specific food guidance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Herbs and formulations" icon="leaf">
    Deep dives into individual herbs, classical Ayurvedic formulas (like Triphala or Chyawanprash), and how preparations differ in potency and use.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Daily and seasonal routines" icon="sun">
    Dinacharya (daily routine) and ritucharya (seasonal routine) practices, including oil pulling, self-massage (abhyanga), and sleep hygiene.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Therapeutic treatments" icon="spa">
    Explanations of Panchakarma procedures, Rasayana (rejuvenation) therapies, and other classical treatment approaches.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mind and consciousness" icon="brain">
    Ayurvedic psychology, the three gunas (sattva, rajas, tamas), and practices for mental and emotional wellbeing.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Video library

Video content is delivered through the `VideoPlayer` component, which supports playback controls, captions, and quality selection.

Types of content available in the video library:

- **Guided practices** — demonstrations of yoga sequences, pranayama (breathwork), and meditation techniques organized by dosha
- **Practitioner talks** — explanations from Ayurvedic physicians covering specific conditions, herbs, or treatment approaches
- **Herb and preparation guides** — step-by-step videos showing how to prepare common Ayurvedic formulations at home
- **Lifestyle walkthroughs** — practical guides to implementing dinacharya and seasonal routines into a modern daily schedule

## How education complements your health plan

The content in the education hub is not standalone — it is designed to give you the vocabulary and context to understand and apply your Mydva consultation results more effectively.

When your consultation recommends a specific herb, a related article explains its classical Ayurvedic properties and how it works in the body. When your lifestyle recommendations include abhyanga (self-massage with oil), a video demonstrates the technique. This layered approach means your health plan becomes more actionable the more you engage with the education content.

<Info>
Articles and videos referenced in your consultation results are surfaced directly in the recommendation sections where relevant, so you can follow up on specific guidance without having to search for it manually.
</Info>

## Suggested learning path for beginners

If you are new to Ayurveda, starting with foundational concepts will help you get the most from every other feature in Mydva.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete the dosha quiz first">
    Before reading about doshas in detail, take the [dosha analysis quiz](/features/dosha-analysis). Understanding your own constitution makes the theoretical content immediately relevant and easier to remember.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the dosha fundamentals articles">
    Start with the articles on your primary dosha. Learn its qualities, how it manifests when balanced, and what drives it out of balance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch a daily routine walkthrough">
    The dinacharya video series gives you a concrete set of practices to start applying right away, even before you have a full consultation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request your first personal consultation">
    After building basic familiarity with Ayurvedic concepts, your [consultation results](/features/personal-consultation) will make more sense and be easier to act on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore herbs relevant to your plan">
    Once you have a consultation, use the [herbal remedy explorer](/features/herbal-remedy) alongside the herb articles and preparation videos to understand what you have been recommended and why.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
The education hub adds new articles and videos regularly. Check back after major life events — a seasonal change, a health shift, or a new consultation — to find content relevant to where you are now.
</Tip>
